WHY 5 IS PARTIAL AND 6 FAILS:
The per-topic `synchronized` produce/consume monitor on InMemoryPubSubTopic is the binding bottleneck under the canonical Phase 5 winning config (WC=off + RMD cache=on, 100k keys, multi-producer N=2). MockInMemoryProducerAdapter.sendMessage() returns synchronously after a single append; one producer thread can fire ~200k records/sec. With 4+ producer threads × 2 regions plus per-server consumer threads + admin/version-topic consumers all contending on the same monitor, the consumer's drain rate (~100-120k records/sec/server) is ~10x slower than the producer's input rate. The per-iteration sentinel (sent at the end of runPutWorkload()) is buried behind millions of older records and never becomes visible within the teardown timeout. Even an empty push (one record from each region!) takes >5 minutes to drain because the system-store creation chain serializes through the same monitor.

The proposed fix (Phase 9): replace `synchronized produce(int partition, …)` and `synchronized consume(int partition, …)` on InMemoryPubSubTopic with a per-partition `Lock[]` array (one ReentrantLock per partition). Producers and consumers operating on different partitions then proceed independently. Estimated effort: half a day in InMemoryPubSubTopic.java, no API surface change. Cross-cutting test sweep needed (StoreIngestionTaskTest's 318 cases were exercised in Phase 8 under the existing synchronized contract and may rely on the all-partition serialization for ordering guarantees).
